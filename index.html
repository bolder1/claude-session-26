What you're actually working with

It isn't a magic output machine. It's a forgetful genius.

Claude is the most talented, fastest junior you've ever hired — and it ships about 70% of the way there. Read the label before you cook with it.

Ingredient 01raw

No context.

It has never seen your product, your users, or your taste. The plate arrives empty.

Ingredient 02volatile

No memory.

An hour into a chat, it's forgotten how the chat began. Long threads quietly get dumber.

Ingredient 0370% done

No taste.

It hands you seventy percent. The last thirty — spacing, hierarchy, judgment — is the reason you have a job.

03

The method

The whole job now fits in three moves.

Stop making pixels. Start directing them. Each move gets demoed live — then you run it yourself.

Move 01

Brief it like a junior.

Context, constraints, references, the format you want back. You already know how to write a good creative brief — that's your unfair advantage.

Move 02

Let it produce.

At a speed you can't match. Screenshots become critiques; briefs become working React, rendering while you watch. Treat the first output as a sketch, never a comp.

Move 03

Judge it like a director.

Spacing, hierarchy, brand, the call only you can make. That's the thirty percent. That's the point. Then: new task, new chat — context is currency.
